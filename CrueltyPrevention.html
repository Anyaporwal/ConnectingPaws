Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960

Section 11: Acts Considered as Cruelty (Punishable Offenses)

11(1)(a) - Beating, kicking, over-riding, over-driving, over-loading, or torturing any animal
11(1)(b) - Unnecessarily mutilating any animal or performing painful operations without anesthesia
11(1)(c) - Confinement without adequate food/water (causing starvation/thirst)
11(1)(f) - Keeping animals in dirty/enclosed spaces leading to breathing difficulties
11(1)(g) - Using injured/sick animals for labor
11(1)(l) - Phooka (artificially inflating animal genitals to increase milk production)
11(1)(m) - Tail docking (cutting tails) or ear cropping for cosmetic purposes
11(1)(e) - Keeping animals chained for long periods without movement
11(1)(h) - Abandoning any animal without care
Section 12 - Organizing/participating in animal fights (e.g., dogfights, cockfights)

Penalties (Section 11)

First offense: Fine up to ₹50

Subsequent offenses: Fine up to ₹100 and/or 3 months imprisonment

(Note: These penalties are widely criticized as outdated and inadequate.)
