Pet and Animal Protection Laws in India

1. Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act (1960)

Section 11 - Cruelty Definitions

  • 11(1)(a): Beating, kicking, over-riding, over-driving, over-loading animals
  • 11(1)(b): Unnecessary mutilation or painful operations without anesthesia
  • 11(1)(c): Confinement without adequate food/water
  • 11(1)(e): Keeping animals chained for long periods
  • 11(1)(h): Abandoning any animal without care
  • Section 12: Organizing animal fights (dogfights, cockfights)

Penalties

  • First offense: Fine up to ₹50
  • Subsequent offenses: Fine up to ₹100 and/or 3 months imprisonment

2. Animal Birth Control (ABC) Rules (2001)

  • Sterilization and vaccination of stray dogs mandatory
  • Culling of stray dogs prohibited
  • Municipalities must implement ABC programs
  • Relocation of dogs only permitted for medical reasons

3. Pet Shop Rules (2018)

  • Mandatory registration of all pet shops
  • Minimum space requirements for animals
  • Proper veterinary care must be provided
  • Sale of sick or unweaned animals prohibited
  • Records of all sales/purchases must be maintained

4. Indian Penal Code Provisions

  • Section 428: Mischief by killing/maiming animals worth ₹10+ (2 years jail)
  • Section 429: Mischief by killing/maiming cattle/elephants worth ₹50+ (5 years jail)

5. Transport Rules

  • Maximum 6 hours continuous transport without rest/water
  • Proper ventilation and space mandatory
  • Sick/pregnant animals cannot be transported

6. Municipal Laws (Vary by City)

  • Dog licensing requirements
  • Leash laws in public places
  • Vaccination requirements (especially rabies)
  • Noise/nuisance regulations
